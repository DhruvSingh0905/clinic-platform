# The buyer

## Who: physique/bodybuilding coaches
Independent online coaches running rosters of dedicated physique athletes — prep coaches, "enhanced" coaches, online bodybuilding coaches. Typically solo or a small team, monetized via monthly coaching fees per client. They already run check-ins and programming through generic tools and texting; they have no consolidated data view.

## Why this customer (vs. the earlier clinic target)
- **Adherence:** their clients are self-selected dedicated; passive sync and assume-adherence are more reliable.
- **Reachable, fast deciders:** coaches live on Instagram/Discord, buy their own tools, decide individually in days — not procurement cycles.
- **Founder fit:** native credibility and warm-intro access to this exact world. The single biggest GTM advantage and the main reason the pivot is easier to break into.

## Who actually signs
The coach. One person, their own money, their own decision. The pitch is: *"see everything your clients generate between check-ins, in one place, with the stuff that matters flagged — so you coach better and faster and they feel it."* Better coaching + client retention, not data plumbing.

## What they care about
- Coaching more clients well without more hours (the roster queue saves time)
- Looking sharp to clients — catching a trend before the client flags it
- Client retention (the felt sense of being closely watched)
- Their existing workflow not being ripped out — we consolidate data, we don't replace their programming tool necessarily

## The substance line, from the buyer's view
Coaches will *want* substance management in-app because it's part of what they do. The product deliberately doesn't offer it as a coach tool — the user self-logs, the coach views read-only, substance conversations happen off-platform (see [[wiki/principles]] and [[wiki/architecture/coach-read-layer]]). Expect this to be the most-requested missing feature. Holding the line is a product decision, not a gap to close. The data value stands without it (removal test).

## The load-bearing uncertainty about the buyer
Do physique coaches feel data-fragmentation pain acutely enough to *pay*, or do they get by fine on screenshots and Sunday check-in forms? Adherence and GTM are easier than the clinic version, but this is still the question that gates the build, and it's answerable only by talking to coaches. See [[wiki/ops/customer-discovery]].

## Pricing direction (untested)
Likely per-coach subscription, possibly banded by roster size. Pricing power comes from time saved + retention lift. Market sizing and ceiling are open — see [[wiki/ops/market]]. The founder is fine with a niche.
