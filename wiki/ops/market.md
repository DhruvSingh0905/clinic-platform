# Market

## Segment
Independent physique/bodybuilding coaches running rosters of dedicated (often enhanced) athletes — prep coaches, online bodybuilding coaches. Solo or small teams, monetized via monthly per-client coaching fees. They run programming and check-ins through generic tools + texting and have no consolidated client-data view.

## Why this segment
- The enhanced-athlete data is exactly what CDE already models — the moat transfers directly.
- Coaches are reachable, self-fund their tools, and decide individually and fast.
- Adherence is favorable: paying physique clients are dedicated by selection.

## Competitive backdrop
- **Generic coaching platforms** (Trainerize, TrueCoach, etc.) — programming delivery, check-in forms, habit/macro tracking. They do NOT consolidate health/wearable/lab data or do enhanced-athlete-aware detection. We are not a coaching-workflow tool; we're the data-intelligence layer alongside whatever they use.
- **Consumer self-tracking apps** (incl. AAS cycle trackers on the App Store) — single-user, no coach view. They prove the *self-logging* model is viable; they don't address the coach's consolidation problem.
- So the wedge is real: nobody gives a coach one domain-aware view across all their clients' health data.

## Size and ceiling (open, be honest)
Sizing is not yet done. The population of serious physique coaches is a niche; pricing power comes from time saved + retention lift, likely per-coach (possibly roster-banded) subscription. This is almost certainly profitable-niche SaaS, not venture-scale — and the founder is explicitly fine with a niche. Do not inflate it. Real sizing is a customer-discovery and desk-research task before any scale assumptions.

## Why augmentation, not replacement
Coaches won't drop Trainerize for an unproven vendor. They might add a layer that consolidates client data and flags what matters — additive, low switching cost. Lead with "see everything in one place," not "replace your stack."

## The substance line as market positioning
The product deliberately does not offer coach-side substance management; the user self-logs, the coach views read-only (see [[wiki/principles]], [[wiki/architecture/coach-read-layer]]). This will be the most-requested missing feature. It is a held line, not a roadmap gap — and the data-consolidation value stands without it (removal test). How loudly coaches push on this is itself a discovery signal worth capturing.

## What still needs validating (not desk-researchable)
Whether coaches feel the fragmentation pain acutely enough to pay, and how absolute the demand for in-app substance management is. See [[wiki/ops/customer-discovery]].
