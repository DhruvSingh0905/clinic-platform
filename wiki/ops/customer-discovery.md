# Customer discovery

## The gate
The build is gated on this. The load-bearing uncertainty: **do physique coaches feel data-fragmentation pain acutely enough to pay — or do they get by fine on screenshots and Sunday check-in forms?** No screen answers this. 5 real coach conversations do. (Secondary question to listen for: do they immediately demand substance management, and how do they react to "your client logs it, you view it"?)

## The trap to avoid
A polished demo makes coaches polite, not honest. They'll say "sick" and you'll mistake it for validation. The script below is designed to *falsify* — to surface "I'm fine without this" if that's the truth — rather than lead the witness to "yes."

## Call script (don't lead the witness)
Open with their world, not your idea. Do NOT pitch until the back half.

**Their current reality (no mention of your product):**
1. Walk me through how you track a client between check-ins today. What do you actually look at, and when?
2. How many clients are you coaching right now? Does that feel like headroom or a lot?
3. Tell me about the last time a client had something going on (overreaching, a bad bloodwork marker, recovery tanking) that you wished you'd caught sooner. What would've had to be true to catch it?
4. How do you handle their data now — screenshots, spreadsheets, them telling you? What's annoying about it?

**Pain-reality probe (the falsification core):**
5. If you had every client's Whoop + nutrition + labs in one place with the stuff that matters flagged — would you use it, or is your current way good enough? (Listen for "good enough" — that's the inversion.)
6. What would make you NOT adopt something like this?

**Only now, the concept:**
7. [Show the mock / CDE output.] Here's a ranked list of which clients need eyes this week, with the reason. Useful, or noise?
8. When in your week would you actually open this?
9. Where does it fit with what you use now (Trainerize/TrueCoach, texting, sheets)?

**The substance question (let them raise it; if they do):**
10. [If they ask about managing cycles in-app] The client logs their own protocol and you see it read-only; cycle conversations stay in your DMs. Does that work for how you operate, or is it a dealbreaker? (Their answer tells you how loud the demand for the line you won't cross will be.)

**Willingness to pay (soft):**
11. If this saved you time and helped retention, what would make it worth paying monthly? (Don't anchor; let them.)

## What counts as signal
- **Confirm:** unprompted stories of missed things; "my data is a mess"; "I'd open this every Sunday."
- **Invert:** "screenshots are fine"; "I don't have time to look at more"; "my clients wouldn't sync." If 3+ of 5, the pain isn't acute enough — rethink before building.
- **Watch:** "I need to manage their cycle in here or it's useless." If this is universal and absolute, the defensible product may not be the one coaches want — a real signal to sit with, not engineer around.

## Sourcing the calls
Physique/online coaches in the founder's network and adjacent (Instagram/Discord reach). Founder's credibility in this world is the warm-intro asset. Outreach style per founder's known preferences: concise, specific hook, direct, humble-confident.

## Note on the founder's bias
The founder is an enhanced athlete and systems engineer who would personally love this tool. That is not evidence a coach will pay for it. Hold that suspicion through every call — the founder is close to the user, not the buyer.
